The big switch to Mercury

What is the big switch to Mercury?

The big switch to Mercury is the critical period of time when we decommission our current applications and transfer data into Mercury, EY’s new global enterprise solution. The big switch to Mercury commenced 6 September and lasts until Mercury go-live on 14 October. It impacts all people in the UK and Ireland.

To prepare for Mercury, important information will be sent to you over the coming weeks, which will explain the actions you must take. This will be done via weekly emails and will be highlighted on this website, as and when they become relevant. Remember to regularly return to this portal to ensure you are up-to-date with these actions and familiar with application blackout dates.

What is blackout?

From 8pm on 6 September until go-live on 14 October, there is a phased application “blackout”. During this time, current applications (e.g. GFIS, InterAction, gT&E) are decommissioned and data is transferred into Mercury. Once this process begins, access to these applications will be either removed or restricted to read-only.

To view the full list of impacted applications and blackout dates, see the timeline below.




What are blackout procedures and offline templates?

During blackout, you are required to follow interim blackout procedures and use offline templates. This is to ensure that the business can continue to operate and that critical information is retained. Blackout impacts everyone, and you must use offline templates to record your data so that you can key it into Mercury once live.

The blackout interim procedures and offline templates are available here. During blackout, your offline templates should be stored in a safe place, as defined by your Service Line.

After Mercury go-live on 14 October, you will need to key the information captured in the offline templates into Mercury yourself. It is important to note that while Mercury will go-live on 14 October, you will be able to enter your time into Mercury starting from 7 October.


Your responsibilities during the big switch to Mercury

Regardless of your role, you will need to participate in these preparation activities for blackout and Mercury go-live.

  1. Read the weekly big switch to Mercury emails to learn what actions you need to take
  2. Review the blackout timeline above and identify which applications undergoing blackout will have an impact on you
  3. Check this website regularly for updates and information during the big switch to Mercury

The big switch FAQs

What is Cutover?

Cutover is the project name for the big switch. It includes the planning process and final execution of data or application conversions into Mercury. The process is designed to minimise any disruption caused by the implementation and/or by new business processes while data is being migrated. It focuses on making sure:

  1. The entire solution is ready for transition to new ways of working
  2. Information is converted correctly to the new system, and
  3. The transition is managed effectively through go-live and post go-live.

What happens during Cutover?

During Cutover, data from current applications is captured and loaded into Mercury. For this to happen, there will be an application freeze or blackout period. Depending on the amount of data, it could take up to several weeks before data is completely loaded. During this time a manual procedure may be needed to capture new data or account for changes to the data during the Cutover time frame.

What is data conversion?

Data conversion is the process that includes the extraction of data from the current application and transforming and loading it into the new Mercury solution.

Why are there multiple blackout dates?

EY’s existing applications (such as GFIS and gT&E) will be taken offline over a period of four weeks. Each application has its own blackout window, relating to the respective complexity and volume of the data required to be transferred to Mercury.

What are blackout procedures and templates and where can I find them?

Blackout procedures are the interim procedures that will enable you to complete your Business-As-Usual (‘BAU’) activities during the period when legacy applications are unavailable. These procedures should be followed, as instructed, until Mercury goes live on 14 October.
Procedures and templates are available here.

Where should offline templates be stored?

Offline templates should be stored in a safe and accessible place, as defined by your Service Line.

What do I need to do with my offline templates after blackout?

The Mercury go-live on 14 October will conclude the blackout period. This will enable ‘BAU’ activities to be performed in Mercury. You will need to re-key your offline template data into Mercury in a timely manner.

Beginning 7 October, you are required to enter 2 timesheets:

  • gT&E blackout week: 30 Sep – 6 Oct
  • Mercury go-live week (for time only): 7 - 13 Oct

The actions you need to take during the big switch to Mercury will be communicated to you in two ways:

  1. You will receive weekly big switch to Mercury emails detailing the most relevant updates, and emphasising the critical actions required of you.
  2. You will be able to find a complete list of the actions that you need to take here as and when they become relevant

What training do I need to take during the big switch to Mercury?

The training required to prepare yourself for the big switch to Mercury is available on the My Learning page.
