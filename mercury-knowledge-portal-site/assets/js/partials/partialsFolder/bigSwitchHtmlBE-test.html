What is the Big Switch to Mercury?

The big switch to Mercury represents an important part of deploying Mercury and it will impact everyone in UK&I. During the big switch, we are migrating business processes and converting data from the systems we currently use to Mercury. We will also experience a blackout when some of our systems will not be available. This is essential in order for the UK&I to move to Mercury and to have access to current data and information, so regular business activities can resume in Mercury after go-live on 14 October (early time entry in Mercury begins on 7 October).

Blackout begins on 14 September until go-live on 14 October. You can view the full list of system blackout dates here (insert link).

The business will continue to operate during the big switch to Mercury. But in some cases, we will be reliant on offline templates for you to continue your business-as-usual (‘BAU’) activities.

What is your responsibility during the big switch to Mercury?

Regardless of your role, you will need to participate in a number of preparation activities for blackout and go-live.

  • Read the Mercury updates email from the big switch mailbox
  • Review the blackout timeline (link) and identify the impacts on you
  • Prepare your deliverables for blackout
  • Understand how to work during blackout - what will/won’t you be able to do
  • If you are working on cross-border engagements, find out more on what you need to do on the co-existence page here

The big switch FAQs

What happens during the Big Switch to Mercury?

During the Big Switch to Mercury, process data from current systems is captured and loaded into the new system. For this to happen, there may need to be a system freeze or blackout period. Depending on the amount of data, it could take up to several days before data is completely loaded. During this time a manual procedure may be needed to capture new data or account for changes to the data during blackout.

What is data conversion?

Data conversion is the process that includes the extraction of data from the current system and transforming and loading it to the new Mercury system.

Why are there multiple blackout dates?

Blackout dates are broken down into two types: a master data load component and a transactional load component. Different types of data are impacted during these two periods and each will have different freeze/blackout activities associated with them. Freeze/blackout activities and impacts will be communicated by to end users.
